Key Terminology

Sewer Camera Inspection
A non-invasive diagnostic tool used to inspect the interior of sewer lines and detect blockages or damage.
Hydro Jetting
A high-pressure water cleaning technique used to clear stubborn clogs and clean the inside of pipes.
Leak Detection
The process of identifying hidden water leaks within plumbing systems, often using specialized equipment like sonar or infrared cameras.
Trenchless Sewer Replacement
A minimally invasive method for replacing damaged sewer lines without the need for extensive digging.
Water Heater Repair
The service of fixing malfunctioning or inefficient water heating systems in homes and businesses.

Common Questions (FAQ)

1. How do I know if I need a sewer camera inspection?

If you're experiencing frequent drain backups or slow drainage, a sewer camera inspection can help identify underlying issues like blockages or tree root intrusion.

2. How does hydro jetting work?

Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water to clear blockages and clean the interior of your pipes, making it an effective solution for stubborn clogs and debris buildup.

3. What should I do if I have a water leak in my home?

It's important to act quickly by turning off the water supply and contacting a professional plumber. Early leak detection can save you from costly water damage.
